In planning for great power contests, political-military leaders need to consider their strategies for deterrence of conflict, for warfighting operations should deterrence fail, for escalation management, and for war termination. Planners typically focus on warfighting, while strategists invest much of their time on postures aimed at deterring conflict in specific contingencies. Preciously little attention is paid to the question of escalation management, which should take priority in contests between major nuclear powers. Thinking on war termination is similarly nascent. This gap is in part born of a sustained great power interregnum since World War II, but also stems from the lack of attention given this subject in the post-Cold War period.
