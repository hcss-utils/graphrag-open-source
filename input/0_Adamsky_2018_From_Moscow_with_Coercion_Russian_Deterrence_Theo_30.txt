The 'asymmetrical approach' employs 'a complex of means unequal to those of the adversary' and may include causing apprehension with regard to the adversary's intentions and responses; demonstration of resolve and capabilities to repulse the invasion with unacceptable consequences; military actions aimed at deterring the potential aggressor by assured destruction of its vulnerable and strategically important objects and persuasion that aggression is doomed to failure. 40 Outlining the coercion mechanism, the GS experts imply both denial and punishment. According to them, both the prevention of aggression and repulsion should combine defensive actions, aimed at defeating the invading forces, with asymmetrical measures, aimed at inflicting unacceptable damage in nonmilitary spheres.
